## What Is Your Dating Voice?

### Your Natural Communication Style

**Your voice is:**
- How you naturally talk
- Your rhythm and pacing
- Your sense of humor (or not)
- Your vocabulary
- How you express interest

### Not a Performance

**Your voice isn't:**
- What you think sounds good
- Copying successful templates
- Trying to be someone else
- Performing a version of yourself

### Recognizable to People Who Know You

**Test:** Would your friends recognize your messages as you?

## Discovering Your Voice

### How Do You Talk to Friends?

**Notice:**
- Your natural conversation style
- How you joke (if you do)
- Your pacing and length
- Topics you gravitate toward
- How you express interest in people

### What Feels Natural?

**Pay attention to:**
- When communication feels easy
- When you're not thinking hard about what to say
- What flows vs. what's forced
- When you feel like yourself

### What Feels Like Performing?

**Notice when:**
- You're trying to seem a certain way
- Messages feel forced
- You're imitating someone else
- You don't feel like yourself

---

## Elements of Voice

### Tone

**Are you naturally:**
- Playful or serious?
- Casual or formal?
- Warm or reserved?
- Enthusiastic or chill?

**Lean into your natural tone.**

### Humor

**Your humor style:**
- Witty and clever?
- Deadpan?
- Silly?
- Not particularly humorous?

**All are valid. Don't force humor if it's not you.**

### Pacing

**Do you naturally:**
- Write longer messages?
- Keep things brief?
- Ask lots of questions?
- Prefer back-and-forth?

### Topics

**What do you naturally:**
- Talk about?
- Find interesting?
- Want to know about others?
- Return to repeatedly?

## Developing Your Voice

### Write and Review

**Practice by:**
- Writing messages without editing
- Then reading them back
- Noticing what sounds like you
- What sounds forced

### Try Different Approaches

**Experiment with:**
- Being more direct
- Being more playful
- Asking different questions
- Different message lengths

**See what feels authentic.**

### Get Feedback

**Ask trusted friends:**
- Does this sound like me?
- What's my communication style?
- What do you notice about how I talk?

## Common Voice Problems

### Trying to Be Clever

**If you're not naturally witty:**
- Don't force it
- Clever attempts that fall flat are worse than genuine simple messages
- Be yourself instead

### Over-Formal

**If you're too formal:**
- It creates distance
- Feels impersonal
- Unless formality is genuinely your style

### Too Casual

**If you're too casual:**
- Might seem low-effort
- Could communicate disinterest
- Unless casual is genuine and matches their energy

### Copying Others

**If you're imitating:**
- It won't sound right
- Creates mismatch with real you
- Not sustainable

## FAQ

**What if my natural voice isn't "attractive"?**
Your natural voice attracts the right people. Performing attracts wrong people. Be yourself and find compatible matches.

**What if I communicate differently with different people?**
Some adaptation is natural. But there's a core voice that's you. Find and lean into that core.

**How do I know if I've found my voice?**
Communication feels easy. You don't have to think hard about what to say. People get an accurate sense of you.

**What if my voice is "boring"?**
Genuine is never boring to the right person. Personality shows through authentic communication. Be yourself.
