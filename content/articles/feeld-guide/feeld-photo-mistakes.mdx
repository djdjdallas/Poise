Not getting matches on Feeld? Your photos might be the problem. Many people make the same mistakes that tank their profiles without realizing it.

Here are the most common photo mistakes and how to fix them.

---

## The Biggest Mistakes

### 1. No Clear Face Photo

**The problem:**
- People can't see what you look like
- Sunglasses in every photo
- Face hidden or obscured
- Too far away to see features

**The fix:**
- At least one clear, well-lit face photo
- Eyes visible (no sunglasses)
- Close enough to see your face
- Nothing blocking your features

### 2. Only One Photo

**The problem:**
- Might be catfishing
- No sense of who you are
- Limited information to judge
- Signals low effort

**The fix:**
- 4-6 photos minimum
- Variety of angles and contexts
- Different aspects of yourself
- Shows you're real and invested

### 3. Old/Outdated Photos

**The problem:**
- Not what you look like now
- Setting up disappointment
- Dishonest representation
- People will notice when meeting

**The fix:**
- Photos from last 6-12 months
- Accurate to current appearance
- Update when you change significantly
- Be someone they'll recognize

---

## Misleading Photos

### Heavy Filters

**The problem:**
- Doesn't look like you
- Beauty/face-altering filters
- Creates unrealistic expectation
- Signals insecurity

**The fix:**
- Natural photos
- Minor editing okay (brightness, crop)
- No face-changing filters
- Look like the real you

### Strategic Angles Only

**The problem:**
- Same flattering angle every photo
- Hiding your appearance
- People suspect deception
- Creates in-person surprise

**The fix:**
- Variety of angles
- Full body included
- Honest representation
- Set accurate expectations

### Ancient Photos

**The problem:**
- You've aged significantly
- Major appearance changes
- Hair, weight, style different
- Feels like catfishing

**The fix:**
- Recent photos only
- Update regularly
- Be proud of current you
- Attract people who want who you are now

---

## Low Quality Photos

### Blurry/Pixelated

**The problem:**
- Can't see you clearly
- Looks lazy
- Raises suspicions
- Hurts overall impression

**The fix:**
- Clear, high-resolution photos
- Check quality before uploading
- Retake if necessary
- Quality matters

### Bad Lighting

**The problem:**
- Too dark to see
- Harsh shadows
- Washed out
- Unflattering

**The fix:**
- Natural light when possible
- Even, flattering lighting
- Not backlit
- Face clearly visible

### Terrible Backgrounds

**The problem:**
- Messy room visible
- Dirty mirror
- Bathroom selfie
- Distracting environment

**The fix:**
- Clean, neutral backgrounds
- Attractive or interesting settings
- Nothing embarrassing visible
- Environment adds, not detracts

## Category Specific Mistakes

## Quick Fixes

### If You Have No Good Photos

**Do this:**
1. Ask a friend to take some this weekend
2. Good lighting + nice outfit + relaxed
3. Take 50, select 5
4. Update profile immediately

### If You're Not Sure What's Wrong

**Try:**
- Ask trusted friends for honest feedback
- Compare to profiles that attract you
- Remove weakest photo and watch results
- Test different first photos

### If Nothing's Working

**Consider:**
- Complete photo refresh
- Professional photos (casual style)
- Different angles and contexts
- Making sure photos match bio energy

## FAQ

**How do I know if my photos are the problem?**
If you're getting few matches but good conversations once matched, photos might be fine. If you're getting matches but bad responses, bio might be the issue. If nothing works, start with photos.

**Should I get professional photos taken?**
It can help, but make sure they look natural, not like headshots. The goal is high quality candids, not corporate portraits.

**Is one bad photo hurting my whole profile?**
Yes, potentially. People often judge by worst photo. Remove anything that doesn't serve you.

**How often should I update photos?**
Every 6-12 months, or whenever your appearance changes significantly. Fresh photos can also reset your profile in the algorithm.
