How someone communicates on Feeld tells you a lot about how they'll be in person. Learning to read green and red flags in conversation helps you invest in the right connections.

Here's what to watch for.

---

## Green Flags

### They Actually Read Your Profile

**What it looks like:**
- References specific things you wrote
- Asks about interests you mentioned
- Comments on something beyond your photos

**Why it matters:**
- Shows genuine interest in you as a person
- Indicates attention and effort
- Suggests they're looking for compatibility, not just attraction

**Example:**
> "I saw you mentioned you're into hikingâ€”have you done any good trails lately?"

### They Ask Questions

**What it looks like:**
- Shows curiosity about your life
- Follows up on things you share
- Wants to understand you better

**Why it matters:**
- Conversation is reciprocal
- They're interested in connection, not just validation
- Indicates emotional intelligence

**Example:**
> You: "I had a busy week at work."
> Them: "What kind of work do you do? Sounds intense."

### They Share About Themselves

**What it looks like:**
- Opens up when you ask
- Offers information without interrogation
- Has specific details and stories

**Why it matters:**
- They're engaged in building connection
- Willing to be vulnerable
- Not hiding or being evasive

**Example:**
> "I've been exploring ENM for about two years now. My partner and I opened up after a lot of conversations about what we both wanted."

### They Respect Your Pace

**What it looks like:**
- Doesn't push for personal info too fast
- Comfortable with your response time
- No pressure to meet immediately

**Why it matters:**
- Shows they understand consent and boundaries
- Indicates patience and security
- Suggests they'll respect you in person

**Example:**
> "No rush on the video callâ€”whenever works for you."

### They Handle "No" Well

**What it looks like:**
- Accepts boundaries without pushing back
- Doesn't try to negotiate or guilt
- Continues conversation normally after

**Why it matters:**
- Critical indicator of how they handle consent
- Shows emotional maturity
- Predicts how they'll be in relationship

**Example:**
> You: "I'm not really into that."
> Them: "Totally understand. What does interest you?"

### They're Clear About What They Want

**What it looks like:**
- Honest about their situation
- Clear about what they're looking for
- No mixed messages

**Why it matters:**
- You can actually assess compatibility
- Less likely to have misunderstandings
- Shows self-awareness and integrity

**Example:**
> "I'm looking for ongoing connections, not one-night things. I have a nesting partner and we date separately."

### They Communicate Like an Adult

**What it looks like:**
- Full sentences, not just emoji responses
- Can discuss emotions and desires
- Expresses themselves clearly

**Why it matters:**
- ENM requires strong communication
- Indicates they can handle complexity
- Suggests productive conflict resolution

## Red Flags

### Immediate Sexual Pressure

**What it looks like:**
- Goes sexual before establishing any connection
- Asks for explicit content right away
- Treats you as a fantasy fulfillment

**Why it matters:**
- Doesn't see you as a person
- Likely to push boundaries in person
- Usually looking for very transactional encounters

**Example:**
> First message: "So what are you into? ðŸ˜"

**How to respond:**
> "I'm happy to discuss that stuff eventually, but I like to get to know someone first."

### Refuses Basic Information

**What it looks like:**
- Won't share first name
- Vague about everything
- Gets defensive when asked normal questions

**Why it matters:**
- May be hiding something significant
- Makes vetting impossible
- Suggests secrecy issues

**How to respond:**
> "I need at least basic info to feel comfortable continuing this conversation."

### Love Bombing

**What it looks like:**
- Intense affection immediately
- "I've never felt this connection"
- Fast-tracking emotional intimacy

**Why it matters:**
- Often manipulation tactic
- Can indicate unhealthy attachment
- Scammer behavior pattern

**Example:**
> Day 2: "I think you might be my soulmate."

### Partner Doesn't Know

**What it looks like:**
- "Don't worry about my partner"
- Secretive about their relationship
- Wants to meet when partner is away

**Why it matters:**
- This isn't ethical non-monogamy
- You could be part of cheating
- Indicates dishonesty patterns

**How to respond:**
> "I only connect with people whose partners are fully aware and consenting."

### Gets Angry at Questions

**What it looks like:**
- Defensive when you ask normal things
- "Why do you need to know that?"
- Treats reasonable vetting as insult

**Why it matters:**
- Will likely be defensive in relationship
- May have something to hide
- Poor conflict resolution skills

**Example:**
> You: "What's your relationship structure like?"
> Them: "Why are you interrogating me?"

### Inconsistent Information

**What it looks like:**
- Stories don't add up
- Different answers to same questions
- Vague when pressed for details

**Why it matters:**
- May be lying about something
- Indicates dishonesty pattern
- Trust issues from the start

### Pressures Moving Off Platform

**What it looks like:**
- Immediately wants your number
- Pushes for WhatsApp/Telegram
- Insists on meeting right away

**Why it matters:**
- May be trying to avoid moderation
- Could be scammer behavior
- Rushing past normal vetting

**How to respond:**
> "I prefer to chat here a bit first. If that doesn't work for you, we might not be compatible."

### Dismisses Your Boundaries

**What it looks like:**
- Pushes back on limits you set
- Tries to negotiate your comfort level
- "Just try it" or "Don't be so uptight"

**Why it matters:**
- Will push boundaries in person
- Doesn't respect consent
- Dangerous pattern

**How to respond:**
Unmatch. No explanation needed.

### Only Talks About Themselves

**What it looks like:**
- Never asks about you
- Conversation is one-sided
- Interested in audience, not connection

**Why it matters:**
- Will likely be selfish partner
- Not interested in reciprocal relationship
- Indicates narcissistic tendencies

## Yellow Flags (Worth Watching)

### Slow Responses

**Could mean:**
- Busy life (common in ENM)
- Multiple conversations
- Not that interested

**Watch for:**
- Do they engage when they respond?
- Is the pattern consistent?
- Do they explain or just disappear?

### New to ENM

**Could mean:**
- Learning curve ahead
- Might not know what they want
- Could be great, just inexperienced

**Watch for:**
- Are they doing the work to learn?
- Do they ask thoughtful questions?
- Any signs of couple privilege?

### Very New Profile

**Could mean:**
- Fake account
- Just exploring
- Privacy-conscious new user

**Watch for:**
- Are they willing to verify?
- Do they seem genuine in conversation?
- Any bot behaviors?

### Couple Messaging Together

**Could mean:**
- Healthy unified approach
- Unicorn hunting
- One person controlling the account

**Watch for:**
- Both voices present?
- Interest in you as individual?
- Treating you as person, not fantasy?

## How Flags Interact

### Green Flags + Yellow Flags = Proceed with Caution

If someone shows good signs but also yellow flags:
- Don't dismiss them immediately
- Ask questions about the yellow areas
- See if green flags persist

### Red Flags + Green Flags = Red Wins

Even good qualities don't cancel red flags:
- Boundary violations matter regardless
- Dishonesty affects everything
- Don't rationalize red flags away

### Multiple Red Flags = Leave

One red flag might be a misunderstanding. Multiple means pattern:
- Trust the accumulation
- Don't try to fix them
- Unmatch and move on

## Trusting Your Instincts

### When Something Feels Off

- You don't need to identify the flag
- Discomfort is information
- You don't owe explanations

### When You Want to Give Benefit of Doubt

- Ask clarifying questions
- See how they respond to being questioned
- One chance is enough; patterns don't deserve more
